ABOUT

I first entered the tech community in 2008 when I stumbled into a technical recruitment role and quickly found myself in over my head and completely lost in most conversations. Some incredibly talented developers in the community took pity on me and taught me the verbiage and nuances of the different tech stacks. I found it all very interesting but was content to only know what I needed to in order to not sound like a complete moron when speaking with candidates and managers.

In 2009 I was peppering one of my SME’s with questions over coffee when he laid an old, worn out paperback book on the table and said “Here. Just read this.” I graciously accepted the tattered gift thinking great, another tech book that I won’t be able to understand or make it through. It was his 20+ year old copy of The Soul of a New Machine by Tracy Kidder. That book turned my interest into intrigue. I no longer HAD to learn about development, I GOT to learn about it.

After 8 years of talking about coding, I’d developed a pretty serious case of skill envy. So, in 2016 I left recruiting and joined Nashville Software School to lead the Career Development piece of the immersive bootcamp and eventually put my hands on some code as a student in the evening program. Now, after all these years, I get it.. What started out as a necessary interest, then sincere intrigue, now borders on obsession (in the healthiest way possible, in my opinion). I’ve found that thing that I love to do, and learn about, and talk about.
